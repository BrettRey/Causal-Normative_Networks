# Cover Letter — Synthese

Dear Editors,

Please consider "Effective without warrant: Causal-normative networks and the social life of status" (22 pages) as a regular research article.

The paper extends Khalidi's causal-network account of projectible kinds ("Natural kinds as nodes in causal networks," *Synthese* 2018) to social statuses. Its claim is that projectible order in the social domain runs along typed non-causal edges, status-determination and recognition, as well as causal ones, and that displaying these together explains how a status-assignment can be socially effective, institutionally valid, or communally conferred while remaining morally unwarranted. The argument engages Woodward's interventionism ("Methodology, Ontology, and Interventionism," *Synthese* 2015), Epstein's grounding-anchoring framework, Ásta's conferralism, and the causal-modelling debate over discrimination.

A preprint is available at PhilArchive (https://philarchive.org/rec/REYEWW). The manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. Large language models served as drafting and editing aids; the disclosure appears in the acknowledgements on the title page, and I am responsible for all claims and errors.
