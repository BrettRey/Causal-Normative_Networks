# Integrated Revision: Edge Typing, Representation, Separability, and Field Constraints

## Bottom Line

The paper should not claim that intervention-style probes sort four primitive dependence types. That overstates the diagnostic and makes Woodward do work he won't do. The stronger, safer claim is:

> Causal-normative networks faithfully represent real differences among causal production, recognitional representation, and field-grounded status-determination. Status-determination has two graph roles: directed transitions such as undertaking, release, withdrawal, and judgment, and non-directed correlative ties such as duty/claim-right. Counterfactual probes help reveal the projection profile, but they are not a one-step classifier for every edge.

This keeps the paper's contribution: the graph still shows why false recognition, wrongful institutional validity, wrongful communal conferral, and suppressed entitlement differ. It also avoids the vulnerable claim that "normative-transition" is a fourth metaphysical genus.

## Revised Taxonomy

Use three relation-genera and four graph roles.

1. **Causal production / difference-making**
   Recognition, records, sanctions, reliance, demand, exclusion, and self-adjustment can causally affect later responses.

2. **Recognitional representation**
   A belief, record, verdict, or classification purports to track a status, base, or finding. It can fit, misfit, or answer to no status.

3. **Status-determination**
   Field-grounded relations fix whether a status obtains. This genus has two graph roles:

   - **Directed transitions:** undertaking generates duty; release defeats duty; withdrawal terminates permission; judgment creates procedural status.
   - **Non-directed correlatives:** duty and claim-right are one jural relation under two descriptions.

This lets the paper say: transition edges remain real and projectively important, but they are directed status-determination, not causal production and not an extra metaphysical producer.

## Replace the Representational Hedge

Current risk: "representational, not a new layer of metaphysics" can sound like the typing is merely a drawing convention. The repair is:

```latex
The graph is a representation of real dependence relations, not a further social object or an added ground of the statuses it represents. Nora's duty is grounded by her undertaking under the rules of the relevant field; Owen's claim-right is the correlative position; Owen's reliance is an ordinary causal effect; his recognition is a representation of the duty. The realism here is modest but not merely notational: causal production, field-grounded status-determination, Hohfeldian correlation, and recognitional representation have different modal profiles. The network displays those relations together so that projections across them can be tracked.
```

Then replace the transition paragraph with:

```latex
A transition edge is therefore not an extra metaphysical producer of the status. It is a directed status-determination edge: under the field's frame principles, the source item is among the grounds, defeaters, or status-altering conditions for the target status. The network represents that field-grounded relation; it does not replace the grounding story.
```

And soften the division-of-labour concession:

```latex
That rival is right that the graph does not replace grounding and anchoring. It is wrong if it treats the relevant relations as explanatorily separable ledgers whose combination leaves no further projection profile to display.
```

## Repair the Intervention-Probe Claim

Current risk: the manuscript says edge types are "told apart" by intervention or analogous probes. That is too strong. In release-to-no-breach, a Woodwardian could model release as a distinct prior event making a difference to breach-status. The paper's real distinction is not bare difference-making; it is whether the relation is causal production or field-grounded status-determination.

Replace the probe paragraph with:

```latex
The probes do not supply a one-step classifier. Strict Woodwardian intervention applies to causal edges, where independently variable nodes are connected by modular difference-making. Status-rule cases behave differently: release, withdrawal, undertaking, and judgment alter the field conditions under which a status obtains. Their dependence is directed, but it is directed status-determination rather than causal production. Hohfeldian correlatives are the limiting non-directed case: duty and claim-right are not successive statuses but one jural relation under two descriptions.
```

Then revise any sentence saying "modularity sorts the edge types." Use instead:

```latex
The modularity contrast helps locate causal routes and the limiting constitutive cases; the remaining work is done by the field-grounded status-determination relation itself.
```

## Make Regime Formation Principled

Current risk: partial separability looks like a failure exactly in the flagship discrimination case. The fix is to make stage-relativity explicit:

> Partial separability is not a failed diagnostic; it is the diagnostic of regime formation.

Add this in Section 6 before the current no-sharp-threshold paragraph:

```latex
The difference is not frequency by itself. Repeated false recognitions remain false recognition if each episode has to do the whole explanatory work again: one employer misreads a signal, one audience misclassifies a speaker, one official acts on a mistake. A position emerges when earlier uptake becomes part of the practical background for later uptake. Records travel, audiences anticipate one another, gatekeepers treat the ascription as action-guiding, targets adjust before any new decision is made, and sanctions or risks attach to occupying the ascribed place. At that point the pathway is recursive: recognition causes treatment, treatment stabilizes expectations and records, and those stabilized expectations and records help fix the standing to which later recognition responds.

This is why separability is stage-relative. At the episode stage, one can probe the signal-recognition-response pathway while holding standing fixed or absent. At the regime stage, that probe no longer cleanly separates standing from uptake, because the stabilized pathway has become one of the standing's conferral conditions. The loss of clean separability is not a collapse of the distinction between false recognition and wrongful conferral. It is the mark that the case has moved from an episode to a position. The boundary is vague, but it is not unconstrained: a standing node is warranted only when the practice supports projections that no single episode supports, such as altered options, anticipated risk, lowered credibility, institutional routing, and self-adjustment across contexts.
```

This makes the episode-to-position threshold cluster-vague but principled: recursive stabilization by empowered uptake plus new projectible consequences.

## Constrain the Field Parameter

Current risk: field-parameterization can look unfalsifiable. The paper should say what the framework rules out.

Add this after the field-stability paragraph in Section 7:

```latex
The parameter is therefore constrained. Not every useful redescription counts as a field, and not every labelled condition counts as a status. The framework misfires if the alleged field has no stabilized repertoire of moves, statuses, authorities or standing relations, record or uptake routes, and socially intelligible defeaters. It also misfires if the alleged status licenses no projections beyond the case at hand. A one-off regularity, an analyst's convenient grouping, or a label that predicts nothing about recognition, response, constraint, enablement, validity, or defeat is not yet a causal-normative network. Nor may the field parameter be adjusted after the fact merely to save a preferred verdict: changing the field changes the projected routes and defeaters, and those changes must be supported by the practice's rules, records, uptake, or standing relations.
```

This gives the theory failure conditions:

- no stabilized field;
- no projectible status;
- edge-type laundering;
- ad hoc field redescription.

## Global Language Changes

Make these substitutions consistently.

- Replace "four dependence types" with "three relation-genera and four graph roles" where the taxonomy is introduced.
- Replace "normative-transition dependence" with "directed status-determination" in theoretical claims, while allowing "transition edge" as the graph role.
- Replace "constitutive dependence" with "non-directed correlative/status-constitutive tie" where the Hohfeldian case is meant.
- Replace "intervention or analogous counterfactual probes sort the edge types" with "counterfactual probes reveal the projection profile and help distinguish causal routes, status-rule routes, recognition routes, and correlative ties."
- Replace "representational, not metaphysical" with "a representation of real dependence relations, not a further ground or social object."

## Conclusion Rewrite

The conclusion should no longer say:

```latex
The profile becomes explicit when the causal, normative-transition, recognitional, and constitutive routes are displayed together.
```

Use:

```latex
The profile becomes explicit when causal, recognitional, and status-determination routes are displayed together, with status-determination split between directed transitions and non-directed correlatives.
```

This is the revised core claim in one sentence.

## Expected Referee Effect

This revision should neutralize the strongest objection:

- It concedes that Woodwardian intervention is not a universal edge classifier.
- It commits to modest realism about the represented relations.
- It explains why partial separability is predicted in regime cases rather than fatal.
- It gives field-parameterization genuine constraints.

The cost is small but real: the paper no longer advertises four primitive dependence types. The gain is that the taxonomy becomes more honest and harder to collapse.
