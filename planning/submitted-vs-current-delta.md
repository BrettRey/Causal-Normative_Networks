## Major Post-Submission Changes

- Jenkins: §6 now cites ontic injustice and treats it as spanning wrongful institutional validity and wrongful communal conferral. The current draft says Jenkins diagnoses the family of wrongs while the typed network maps and subdivides the profiles.
- Division of labour: §4 and the conclusion no longer claim that separate grounding and causal explanations make some projections unavailable. The claim is now projection-profile perspicuity: separate ledgers can list the facts, but the mixed diagnostic pattern remains implicit.
- Edge taxonomy: the paper now uses three relation-genera displayed by four graph roles: causal production, recognitional representation, and status-determination, with status-determination split into directed status-transition edges and non-directed correlative ties.
- Intervention/probe language: strict Woodwardian intervention is reserved for causal edges. Counterfactual probes reveal projection profiles but are not presented as a one-step classifier for every edge.
- Graph realism: the old "merely representational" hedge is gone. The graph is now a representation of real dependence relations, not a new social object or additional ground.
- Status-determination: §5 now gives the genus a positive mark as determination rather than production. It does not claim that all status-transition edges fail Woodwardian distinctness in the same way.
- Modularity and conferral: §5 and §6 now make separability stage-relative. Loss of clean separability is treated as a mark of movement from episode to position, not as a collapse of the false-recognition/wrongful-conferral distinction.
- Entrenchment threshold: §6 now gives a cluster account of the episode-to-position boundary: recursive uptake, records, anticipatory adjustment, mutual expectation, sanctions, routing, and new projections no single episode supports.
- Field parameter: §7 now states what the framework rules out: non-fields, non-status labels, one-off regularities, and post-hoc field gerrymandering that is unsupported by practice rules, records, uptake, or standing relations.
- Morality as field: the current draft treats moral warrant as an external assessment standpoint rather than validity in a moral field, without giving a first-order theory of morality.
- Almäng: §7 now says explicitly that the typed network adds a route map beyond the separation of institutional status, deontic relation, and institutional function.
- Mechanical/source fixes: possessive citation phrasing was cleaned up, Hu is marked forthcoming, Restatement citations have section numbers, and Hindriks-Guala plus Jenkins entries were added to the public bibliography.

## Practical Constraint

Do not treat the current draft as the submitted manuscript. The current draft is the best starting point for an R&R, but the response letter should not imply that the submitted version already contained these fixes.
