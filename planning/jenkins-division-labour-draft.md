## Tightened Section 4 Reply
Replace the current division-of-labour paragraph in Section 4 with:

```latex
The strongest rival is a division of labour: use grounding and anchoring to explain the status, then use causal mechanisms to explain uptake. That rival is right about metaphysics. The network is not a second ground of the promise, judgment, or discriminatory standing. Its claim is about the projectible profile. The same assignment can support a transition inference, a correlative inference, a recognition inference, and an uptake inference, while failing at a different assessment point. A release cancels the duty even if demand continues. A mistaken verdict can make enforcement and preclusion projectible without making the underlying finding true. A discriminatory standing can be successfully conferred and predict credibility loss, routing, sanction risk, and anticipatory adjustment while remaining morally unwarranted. Separate ledgers can list the facts, but they do not mark the diagnostic pattern: where projection travels, where it changes edge type, and where warrant fails to follow.
```

The stronger claim, then, is not that the separate explanations become false when separated. ## Jenkins Paragraph for Section 6
Insert after the current paragraph ending "A discriminatory standing can be successfully conferred, stabilized by its practice, and morally indefensible at the same time."

```latex
\textcite{jenkins2020OnticInjustice,jenkins2023OntologyOppression} names the central case \term{ontic injustice}: a person is wronged by being socially constructed as a member of a social kind when that construction consists, at least in part, in being subject to wrongful constraints and enablements. That is exactly the regime-side profile I call wrongful communal conferral. The agreement is deliberate. Jenkins shows why the wrong needn't wait for a further bad episode; the standing itself can be injurious because it places the person under a degraded practical position. The present account adds a different piece of machinery. It separates the edge types by which such a position is stabilized: recognitional classifications, causal uptake, constitutive constraints and enablements, and the conferral transition that makes the standing socially real. That separation lets wrongful conferral be compared with false recognition, wrongful institutional validity, and suppressed entitlement without treating them as the same wrong.
```

Lean alternative, if the section needs less literature texture:

```latex
\textcite{jenkins2020OnticInjustice,jenkins2023OntologyOppression} calls this sort of wrong \term{ontic injustice}: a person is wronged by being socially constructed as a member of a social kind when that construction places them under wrongful constraints and enablements. Wrongful communal conferral is meant to occupy that terrain. Jenkins gives the moral-ontological diagnosis; the typed-edge account adds the projection map, separating recognitional classification, causal uptake, constitutive constraint, and successful-but-unwarranted conferral so that this profile can be distinguished from false recognition, wrongful institutional validity, and suppressed entitlement.
```
