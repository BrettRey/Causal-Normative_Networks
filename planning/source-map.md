## Section 1: The Problem of Mixed Explanation

Claim: the central puzzle is a status-assignment that is socially effective, and sometimes field-valid or successfully conferred, without moral warrant.

Primary citations: `khalidi2018nodes`, `hohfeld1913SomeFundamental`.

Use for: the projectibility/network frame and the Hohfeldian constitutive tie.

Avoid: turning the opening into a literature review. The section should stage the puzzle and define the four dependence types.

## Section 2: Why Ordinary Causal Networks Aren't Enough

Claim: Khalidi is right that projectibility requires ordered structure, but moral and institutional cases show that not all order is causal order.

Primary citation: `khalidi2018nodes`.

Use for: ordered causal networks, connected nodes, directionality, and the social-kind/convention contrast.

Avoid: implying Khalidi's view fails in its own target domain. The point is extension pressure, not refutation.

## Section 3: Why Normative Taxonomies Aren't Enough

Claim: a normative taxonomy can classify obligations, permissions, releases, and breaches, but it can't explain projectibility without uptake, recognition, enforcement, reliance, and repair.

Primary citations: `hohfeld1913SomeFundamental`, `khalidi2018nodes`.

Use for: Hohfeldian taxonomy and the list-versus-ordered-structure contrast.

Avoid: treating uptake as constitutive in every case. Some recognition tracks status; some mis-tracks it; some authorized recognitions alter it.

## Section 4: The Typed-Edge Model

Claim: causal-normative networks have typed nodes and typed edges; the heterogeneity is the explanatory point. The graph is an explanatory representation of grounded statuses, recognitions, uptake, and correlative positions, not a replacement for the grounding or anchoring story.

Primary citations: `khalidi2018nodes`, `woodward2003MakingThingsHappen`, `woodward2014MethodologyOntology`, `hohfeld1913SomeFundamental`, `sbisa2023OnIllocutionaryTypes`.

Optional formal analogy: `pullumRogers2008Expressive`, `pullum2001`.

Use for: directed graph inheritance from Khalidi, interventionist edge typing, Hohfeldian constitutive ties, Epstein's grounding/anchoring distinction, Sbisà's deontic-modal effects, and the optional typed-edge/model-theoretic analogy.

Avoid: making the Pullum analogy load-bearing or assuming the readership knows CGEL.

## Section 5: Coupling and Counterfactual Signatures

Claim: this is one network because status-rule, recognition, and authorized-recognition signatures move status and uptake in different patterned ways.

Primary citations: `woodward2003MakingThingsHappen`, `woodward2014MethodologyOntology`, `owens2012ShapingNormativeLandscape`, `raz2022NormativePowers`, `americanLawInstitute1981RestatementContracts`, `americanLawInstitute1982RestatementJudgments`.

Use for: intervention language, Woodward's methodology-not-ontology reply, normative powers, contract/release examples, and the Restatement of Judgments as cautious grounding for the legal consequences of judgments.

Avoid: writing as though interventions on normative status are always physical lab manipulations. The draft now reserves "intervention" for causal edges and uses "signature" for the typed contrasts.

## Section 6: Misrecognition as the Diagnostic Case

Claim: local misrecognition and wrongful conferral come apart. Discrimination is the regime case: a conferred, socially effective, practice-stabilized standing without moral warrant.

Primary citations: `asta2018Categories`, `fraserHonneth2003Redistribution`, `bertrandMullainathan2004EmilyGreg`, `ayres1991FairDriving`, `weinbergerSignalManipulation`, `kohlerHausmann2019EddieMurphy`, `huForthcomingNormativeFactsCausalStructure`.

Use for: conferralism, the recognition/redistribution guardrail, audit-study efficacy, signal manipulation, thick ethical concepts, and normative presuppositions in causal measurement. Audit studies evidence signal-recognition-response pathways; entrenchment and standing do the extra work needed for wrongful conferral.

Current machinery added 2026-05-26: Table 2 separates social efficacy, institutional field-validity, communal successful conferral, recognitional fit, enforceability/finality, and moral warrant. The Figure 2 text now specifies conferring agents, base/ascription, and conferred standing.

Avoid: making discrimination merely a false-belief case. The important regime case is wrongful conferral: a real social standing that lacks moral warrant.

## Section 7: Fields

Claim: a field is an ordinary normative practice or domain, such as contract law, criminal law, medical ethics, ordinary promising, or friendship. It fixes the parameters the network leaves open: status repertoire, validity conditions, authorized agents, recognition channels, records, remedies, and defeaters. A field is the practice relative to which a status supports projections.

Primary citations: `americanLawInstitute1981RestatementContracts`, `americanLawInstitute1982RestatementJudgments`, `sbisa2023OnIllocutionaryTypes`, `epstein2015AntTrap`, `searle1995Construction`, `asta2018Categories`, `boyd1999`, `messick1995Validity`.

Use for: contract-law field examples, judgment field examples, deontic-modal speech acts, Epstein's grounding/anchoring distinction, Searle's institutional status functions, Ásta's institutional/communal conferral contrast, Boyd's field-relative projectibility/accommodation thought, and Messick as a limited analogy for validity as interpretation/use-relative rather than object-in-isolation.

Current machinery added 2026-05-26: §4 now has a compact projection ledger with promise, consent, judgment, and discrimination rows. §7 defines "stable enough" for field-relative projection as recurrence across cases, recognized routes for record or uptake, intelligible defeaters, and continuity between source and target nodes.

Avoid: Bourdieu's technical sense of "field." The draft explicitly uses the term loosely for a normative practice or domain. Avoid making Boyd's HPC account load-bearing here: fields are not HPCs. Avoid making "anchored parameter package" the reader-facing definition. Epstein stays as support for frame principles/anchors; Boyd supports field-relative projectibility; Searle/Ásta carry institutional and communal conferral. Messick is only an analogy, not the source of status validity.

## Section 8: Conclusion

Claim: the framework earns its keep by licensing different projections from different assignments: release projects changed duty, judgment projects procedural consequences, discriminatory standing projects constraints and risk, and none of these projections settles moral warrant.

Primary citations: none unless a specific recapitulation needs one.

Use for: synthesis only.

Avoid: introducing new literature in the conclusion.
