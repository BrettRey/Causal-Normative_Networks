## Section 1: The Problem of Mixed Explanation

Claim: the central puzzle is a status-assignment that is socially effective, and sometimes field-valid, without moral warrant.

Primary citations: `khalidi2018nodes`, `hohfeld1913SomeFundamental`.

Use for: the projectibility/network frame and the Hohfeldian constitutive tie.

Avoid: turning the opening into a literature review. The section should stage the puzzle and define the four dependence types.

## Section 2: Why Ordinary Causal Networks Aren't Enough

Claim: Khalidi is right that projectibility requires ordered structure, but moral and institutional cases show that not all order is causal order.

Primary citation: `khalidi2018nodes`.

Use for: ordered causal networks, connected nodes, directionality, and the social-kind/convention contrast.

Avoid: implying Khalidi's view fails in its own target domain. The point is extension pressure, not refutation.

## Section 3: Why Normative Taxonomies Aren't Enough

Claim: a normative taxonomy can classify obligations, permissions, releases, and breaches, but it can't explain projectibility without uptake, recognition, enforcement, reliance, and repair.

Primary citations: `hohfeld1913SomeFundamental`, `khalidi2018nodes`.

Use for: Hohfeldian taxonomy and the list-versus-ordered-structure contrast.

Avoid: treating uptake as constitutive in every case. Some recognition tracks status; some mis-tracks it; some authorized recognitions alter it.

## Section 4: The Typed-Edge Model

Claim: causal-normative networks have typed nodes and typed edges; the heterogeneity is the explanatory point.

Primary citations: `khalidi2018nodes`, `woodward2003MakingThingsHappen`, `woodward2014MethodologyOntology`, `hohfeld1913SomeFundamental`, `sbisa2023OnIllocutionaryTypes`.

Optional formal analogy: `pullumRogers2008Expressive`, `pullum2001`.

Use for: directed graph inheritance from Khalidi, interventionist edge typing, Hohfeldian constitutive ties, Sbisà's deontic-modal effects, and the optional typed-edge/model-theoretic analogy.

Avoid: making the Pullum analogy load-bearing or assuming the readership knows CGEL.

## Section 5: Coupling and Counterfactual Signatures

Claim: this is one network because status-rule, recognition, and authorized-recognition signatures move status and uptake in different patterned ways.

Primary citations: `woodward2003MakingThingsHappen`, `woodward2014MethodologyOntology`, `owens2012ShapingNormativeLandscape`, `raz2022NormativePowers`, `americanLawInstitute1981RestatementContracts`.

Use for: intervention language, Woodward's methodology-not-ontology reply, normative powers, and the Restatement as legal grounding for judgment/contract examples.

Avoid: writing as though interventions on normative status are always physical lab manipulations. The draft now reserves "intervention" for causal edges and uses "signature" for the typed contrasts.

## Section 6: Misrecognition as the Diagnostic Case

Claim: local misrecognition and wrongful conferral come apart. Discrimination is the regime case: a conferred, socially effective, often field-valid standing without moral warrant.

Primary citations: `asta2018Categories`, `fraserHonneth2003Redistribution`, `bertrandMullainathan2004EmilyGreg`, `ayres1991FairDriving`, `weinbergerSignalManipulation`, `kohlerHausmann2019EddieMurphy`, `huForthcomingNormativeFactsCausalStructure`.

Use for: conferralism, the recognition/redistribution guardrail, audit-study efficacy, signal manipulation, thick ethical concepts, and normative presuppositions in causal measurement.

Avoid: making discrimination merely a false-belief case. The important regime case is wrongful conferral: a real social standing that lacks moral warrant.

## Section 7: Fields

Claim: a field fixes the parameters the network leaves open: status repertoire, validity conditions, authorized agents, recognition channels, records, remedies, and defeaters.

Primary citations: `americanLawInstitute1981RestatementContracts`, `sbisa2023OnIllocutionaryTypes`, `epstein2015AntTrap`, `searle1995Construction`, `asta2018Categories`.

Use for: contract-law field examples, deontic-modal speech acts, Epstein's grounding/anchoring distinction, Searle's institutional status functions, and Ásta's institutional/communal conferral contrast.

Avoid: Bourdieu's technical sense of "field." The draft explicitly uses the term loosely for a normative setting or practice.

## Section 8: Conclusion

Claim: some explanations are unavailable unless causal and normative relations are represented together.

Primary citations: none unless a specific recapitulation needs one.

Use for: synthesis only.

Avoid: introducing new literature in the conclusion.
