# DECISIONS.md - Causal-Normative Networks

Append non-trivial decisions as they are made. Format: `YYYY-MM-DD: Decision. Reason.`

2026-05-24: Split the foundational account into its own paper rather than folding it into the act-kinds paper. Reason: in the act-kinds manuscript the concept is already doing enough work, and a full metaphysics of mixed networks raises distinct objections that deserve standalone treatment.

2026-05-24: Explanandum is the causal-normative network itself, not moral act-kinds. Reason: separates this paper's question (what is such a structure?) from the act-kinds paper's question (why are the kinds projectible?).

2026-05-24: Central opponent is the forced-choice view (either reduce normative status to causal facts, or treat causal talk as metaphor for norms), not essentialism. Reason: essentialism is the act-kinds paper's opponent; this paper's dialectic is anti-reductionist about mixed explanation.

2026-05-24: Organize around four kinds of dependence (causal, constitutive, normative-transition, recognitional) and a typed-edge network model. Reason: this is the mixed-edge structure that Khalidi, Boyd, and Sbisà each leave unprovided.

2026-05-24: Use four carrying cases (promise, consent, official judgment or registration, discrimination). Reason: they triangulate ordinary normative transition, authorized status-creation, and entrenched misrecognition (social efficacy vs moral validity).

2026-05-24: Provisional title "Normative Status in Causal Networks." Reason: journal-friendly; the blunt option ("What Is a Causal-Normative Network?") is good but less venue-friendly. Final title open.

2026-05-24: Reuse verified bibliographic entries from the act-kinds paper's references-local.bib rather than regenerate them. Reason: source-grounding law; the entries were already confirmed against authoritative sources.
